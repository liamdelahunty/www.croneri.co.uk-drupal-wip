Induction training

Good induction training is vital part of the strong foundations on which wise providers build their business. How you prepare and train staff will have a direct impact on your success as a social care service and the wellbeing of the people you support.

In this webinar, we will review the components of a good induction programme and consider how providers can tailor their content to the needs of their service, supporting the learning needs of both new and experienced care staff.

We will also review the changes and improvements that are upcoming later in the year and on into the future.

Key learning points

  • Regulatory background
  • The essential contents of a good induction
  • The Care Certificate
  • Funding
  • Upcoming changes (including the Care Workforce Pathway, the new Adult Social Care Training and Development Fund and new Adult Social Care Certificate)
  • Requirements for trainers
  • Online vs in-person training
  • Competency assessment and observations
  • Tools and resources
  • Gathering feedback and making improvements
  • Getting the best from inspection/assessment

Companies House reforms under ECCTA

Synopsis:

This webinar will help corporates and firms understand why The Economic Crime and Corporate Transparency Act 2023 has been introduced. We will explore Companies House changes coming into force during 2024, their implications, the upcoming filing requirements for small and micro companies, and the proposed new thresholds of the company size classification limits.

We will share the resources available on the Croner-i Navigate research and learning platforms that provide further guidance.

Key learning points:

  • What is ECCTA, and why has it been introduced;
  • The key Companies House changes that came into force on 4 March 2024 and their impact on companies;
  • Proposed changes to the filing requirements of small and micro companies;
  • Introduction to the new offence of failure to prevent fraud; and
  • New thresholds to the Company size classification limits.
